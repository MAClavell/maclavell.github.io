About Me

My name is Michael Clavell. Like everyone else in the industry, I have a great passion for games, but it doesn't lie just in playing them. The who and how behind them is what really gets me excited.

Other than using my mind to craft ideas and tabletop worlds, I've mainly worked in C# with the Unity and Monogame engines, but I also have experience using Lua and Java. Recently I started to learn C++ to go along with my knowledge of Unreal.

My focus lies in gameplay programming and scripting. Taking visions and turning them into tangible experiences is a huge part as to why I do what I do.

Major Projects

RPG Shop UI (In-progress)

RPG Shop UI aims to help GameMasters of any tabletop RPG with the management of shops and major items and upgrades. With this tool, GMs will be able to utilize the power of Unity to easily create shops beforehand that players can browse during gametime.


DIE-PARTISAN

DIE-PARTISAN is a 2D twin stick shooter created by myself and three other RIT students in the Monogame engine. The game focuses on Lucifer as he fights his way through infinite, randomly stitched together levels, battling mysterious rogue angels bent on the destruction of Heaven and Hell.

School Projects

Polygons

Polygons is a reimagining of the classic arcade game, Asteroids. Players must maneuver, dash, and shoot their way through an endless amount of dangerous geometry. .

The project was created in Unity3D, but I was not allowed to use any of Unity's built-in methods for collision detection and movement. Therefore, the assignment showcases custom methods for each of these whose concepts can be used in any language or engine.

Source code and documentation available on GitHub


Humans vs. Zombies

A rumored asteroid landing in the swamps outside a southern city has caused the military to panic, sending out troops to recover the potentially dangerous object. Little did they know that the asteroid carries an alien virus that turns humans into brain-hungry monsters. It is a fight for survival in this exciting simulation of Humans vs. Zombies!

Using Unity3D, I was tasked to create a Humans vs. Zombies simulation using Craig Reynold's steering behaviors as a basis. The agents have their own behaviors: zombies will pursue the closest human, while humans will evade zombies that are chasing them. Humans also have the ability to fight back if they are together in groups of three. The result is an interesting simulation where the outcome can change any second.

Source code and documentation available on GitHub
