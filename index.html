About Me

My name is Michael Clavell. Like everyone else in the industry, I have a great passion for games, but it doesn't lie just in playing them. The who and how behind them is what really gets me excited.

Other than using my mind to create ideas and tabletop worlds, I've mainly worked in C# with the Unity and Monogame engines, but I also have experience using Lua and Java. Recently I started to learn C++ to go along with my knowledge of Unreal.

My focus lies in gameplay programming and scripting. Taking visions and turning them into tangible experiences is a huge part as to why I am in this industry.

RPG Shop UI (In-progress)

RPG Shop UI aims to help GameMasters of any tabletop RPG with the management of shops and major items and upgrades. With this tool, GMs will be able to utilize the power of Unity to easily create shops beforehand that players can browse during gametime.
Click here for a more detailed look.


DIE-PARTISAN

DIE-PARTISAN is a 2D twin stick shooter created by myself and three other RIT students in the Monogame engine. The game focuses on Lucifer as he fights his way through infinite, randomly stitched together levels, battling mysterious rogue angels hellbent on the destruction of Heaven and Hell.
Click here for a more detailed look.


Undertale Muffet Recreation

The Muffet Recreation is a project from Summer 2016 where I recreated the entire Muffet encounter from the game Undertale. The recreation was made using the Lua programming language in the Unitale/CYF engine.
